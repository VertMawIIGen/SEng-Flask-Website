Explore the applications of web programming.

Web Programming is applied universally in many of the technologies that people everywhere interact with and utilize everyday. Web applications, created through web progrmaming, serve as the cornerstone for modern, 21st century life as we know it. But before we can understand where it is applied, we must first understand fundamentally what web programming is used to create: interactive websites.

What even are interactive websites?

Interactive websites are websites that are designed to produce real-time and engaging experiences for the user by allowing them to "interact" with the content within the webpage. This means that, as opposed to static websites, interactive websites are dynamic and respond promptly to user actions, allowing for a two-way communication between the website and the user.
Interactive websites are widely used in today's world as they simplify the process of building engagement with users of the website through the showing of personalized and interactions. Typically, interactive websites contain one or more of the following features:

Feature Explanation
User Interaction Website elements that encourage interaction, such as buttons, forms and navigation elements, generate engagement through the user's active participation in using the website. For instance, the user can enter a form to generate search results for the specific information that they are searching for.
Media Dynamic websites often use interactive media such as videos and images in carousels to further engage users in the website.
Dynamic Content Personalized content allows websites to tailor their experiences to the user through content such as recommendations, thus making them interactive.

and many, many more. These features may seem very familiar for many people, notably because nearly everyone in today's world has had experience with them. Many websites utilize these elements, such as:

  • E-Commerce websites.
  • Social media websites.
  • Online learning platforms.

All about E-Commerce Websites

Electronic Commerce, also more commonly known as E-commerce, refers to the concept of trading good and services over the internet. The term "e-commerce" does not just refer to the trading of items, rather it also refers more broadly to many aspects of the service, such as the electronic provision of support services for customers, such as online customer service, the marketing and developing of goods, and the delivering of products to purchasers. There are different types of e-commerce, based on who orders the goods and services, and who sold the goods and services, they are:

The B2B model refers to when a business markets and sells goods directly to other businesses online. There are different types of B2B companies in the e-commerce ecosystem, including:

  • Manufacturers.
    • Companies outsource their production to third party companies, and purchase the required goods through these companies using the B2B model.
  • Wholesalers
    • Wholesalers are companies that sell products in bulk to other companies, such that the other company can sell the products cheaper to consumers.
  • Distributors
    • Distributors refer to companies that sell marketing services to other companies, allowing companies to pay to advertising their products.
  • Software-as-a-Service Providers
    • Software-as-a-Service businesses sell licenses for software hosted on a cloud to other companies, which then require a monthly payment in order for the company to continue using the product.
An example of the B2B model would be if an electronics company requires a component that they don't produce internally. They would then go and buy the component from other companies, such as manufacturers.

The B2C model involves the selling of goods and services directly to consumers. Currently, this is the most well-known business model, as the general public mostly only interact with this e-commerce model. These companies that follow this model generally fall into the following categories:

  • Direct Sellers
    • Direct sellers are companies from which consumers directly buy from, purchasing either products of the company's own branding, or from other brands. Consumers make online purchases directly from the seller, with an example of such a company being a retailer such as Target.
  • Online intermediaries
    • Online intermediaries are companies that serve as the bridge between potential consumers and companies. They do not own any of the products or services, only serving as the platform or marketplace for buyers and sellers to come together and interact. An example of such a company is Amazon.
  • Fee-based Companies
    • Fee-based companies sell their software products to consumers using a subscription model. Software-as-a-service businesses use this model, as well as other products such as streaming services, like Netflix.

The C2B model involves a consumer offering their products and services to companies, with both the company and the consumer benefiting from the relationship. This model exists when the consumer creates value, and the business consumes the value. This business model is atypical, and not as common as the other business models, as it the consumer that offers their products an services to businesses for payment or other benefits.

An example of this would be when a consumer runs a blogging channel, and offers to advertise a company's product in exchange for funds.

The consumer to consumer model facilitates the transaction of goods between consumers who want to sell and consumers who want to buy through a third-party platform. Companies that follow the C2C model do not sell their own goods, rather they only provide a marketplace for consumers to sell their goods. Companies like Ebay or Gumtree follow this model of e-commerce.

Progressive Web Applications (PWAs)

Progressive Web Applications (PWAs) are special applications that are, in essence, web applications that operate similarly to native applications. They can be thought of as a mix between traditional websites and native applications, combining the accessibility of a website with the user experience of an application. Now, you might be wondering, what even are webs application and native applications?

Web Applications

A web application is an application that is hosted on a web server and can be accessed from a browser through the internet or an intranet. It is not installed and run on the computer, rather its processes are executed on a server which the browser communicates with.

It is important to note that web applications are distinct from traditional websites. This is because they have a higher level of complexity and interactivity, and are designed for interaction by end users, rather than having only static content, which traditional websites have. In web applicatiions, the user is capable of reading and manipulating data, while in traditional websites, the user can only read the data available.

Native Applications

Native applications are those developed specifically for a certain operating system. They are developed in accordance with the nuances of each specific platform, which can include Linux or windows.

How they relate to PWAs.

PWAs bridge the gap between the web applications and native applications by providing web applications with native application functionality, such as sending push notifications and accessing system resources. They work by using a service worker, which allows PWAs to run in the background without the need to reside in the browser. When the application is installed, the service worker, along with the cache, is installed. This allows PWAs to be used offline as the content is loaded from the cache, with the cache being updated whenever internet access in available.
